The Allegory Mechanism

"Let That Be Your Last Battlefield" — two aliens, one black on the left, one black on the right, destroying each other over which side the pigmentation falls on. The racism allegory is legible. The target is fictional. The audience watches two painted aliens and feels they have grappled with American racial hierarchy. They have not. They have grappled with face paint on a planet that does not exist.

Spock absorbs the franchise's racist vocabulary. A fictional species. Scathing comments about pointed ears and green blood land with real social weight — and then dissolve because the target is imaginary. The mechanism allows the show to perform confrontation without requiring the audience to name a real system operating on real people in the real country where the broadcast was received.

The system that produced the inequality the diversity is supposedly transcending — never named, never examined, never exposed.

That is not boldly going. That is a painted backdrop.
