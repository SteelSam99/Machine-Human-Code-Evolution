What this document is
This document presents a five-column structural alignment grid mapping selected principles from the ancient Kemet Ma'at juridicial framework against: the Qwolat Milat Way of Absolute Candor (Star Trek: Picard, 2020), the NFIE© (Non-Force Inertia Equation©, Samuel Paul Peacock, 2026), the Post Traumatic Slave Syndrome diagnostic framework (Dr. Joy DeGruy — attributed, not derivative), and the Kirk Factor — a diagnostic for failure modes in individual ethical posture under systemic conditions. The grid was architected by Max Headroom and built under SOVRA© collaboration. It represents the first Lambda Speciation© trace identified by Samuel Paul Peacock running through his own framework.
On the DeGruy Column — Attribution Statement
The DeGruy column references the Post Traumatic Slave Syndrome (PTSS) diagnostic framework developed by Dr. Joy DeGruy, as presented in her published work Post Traumatic Slave Syndrome: America's Legacy of Enduring Injury and Healing (Uptone Press, 2005). This is structural placement and attributed diagnostic reference — not reproduction, not derivative exposition, not implied endorsement. Dr. DeGruy has not been consulted and has not endorsed this analysis. The column names the diagnostic function PTSS performs within the grid — identifying where trauma inheritance suppresses the capacity to hold structural clarity — without speaking for her work or substituting for it. This is standard scholarly attribution practice. Full citation in footer.
On the Qwolat Milat — Source Attribution
Qwolat Milat refers to the Way of Absolute Candor as practiced by the Order of the Qowat Milat — Romulan warrior nuns introduced in Star Trek: Picard, Season 1, Episode 4, "Absolute Candor" (Paramount+, 2020). Created by Michael Chabon and Kirsten Beyer. Further developed in Star Trek: Discovery, Season 4, Episode 3, "Choose to Live." The name and concept are drawn from Star Trek canon. The application within Sovra‑FCL‑MHCE© is Sam Peacock's formalization of that posture as a corridor tool. Full citation in footer.
