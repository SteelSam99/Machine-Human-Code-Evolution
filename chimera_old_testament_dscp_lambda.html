Textual Event
Old Testament Codification — 600 BCE to 100 AD
Scholarly consensus: much of the Hebrew Bible assembled in the 5th century BCE. Dead Sea Scrolls (3rd century BCE – 1st century CE) show the text was still fluid until canonization around 100 AD. Multiple variant traditions existed simultaneously. The Qumran community accepted books never admitted to canon. The text was not a fixed object — it was a selection process. What was included and what was excluded is itself a structural event.

Archive status: solid. Documented in Dead Sea Scrolls scholarship, Britannica, Notre Dame, Biblical Archaeology Society.
Suppression Event
Sayana's Speed of Light — 14th Century AD
Sayanacharya (c.1311–1387 CE), minister of the Vijayanagara Empire, comments on Rigveda 1:50 — sunlight travels 2,202 yojanas in half a nimesa. Converting ancient units: 185,794 miles/second. Modern value: 186,281.7 miles/second. Deviation: ~0.26%. Bhatta Bhaskara made the same statement in the 10th century, noting it as "an old tradition" — pushing the knowledge claim earlier. The first Western measurement (Ole Rømer) came in 1676 — 300 years later. Sayana's calculation was not adopted into the canon of scientific history. The Kerala School of mathematics developed infinite series 250 years before Newton. None of this entered the "official" scientific timeline.

Archive status: Sayana's calculation is documented and the math checks out. Whether it constitutes conscious measurement or coincidence is, in the opinion of this author, wrongly debated. The suppression from official history is not debated. Re-classifying this as art, or poetry, or religion does not change the fact that the calculation was made.
